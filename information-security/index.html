Information Security

Information Security (InfoSec) is a discipline focused on protecting information in all its forms --- physical (analog, paper, spoken conversations, ...) as well as digital. InfoSec is built around three core principles known as the CIA triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.

Per Wikipedia:

Information security (infosec) is the practice of protecting information by mitigating information risks. It is part of information risk management. It typically involves preventing or reducing the probability of unauthorized or inappropriate access to data or the unlawful use, disclosure, disruption, deletion, corruption, modification, inspection, recording, or devaluation of information. Protected information may take any form, e.g., electronic or physical, tangible (e.g., paperwork), or intangible (e.g., knowledge).

Given this definition of Information Security, it is appropriate to regard information security as a more general case of Cyber Security, which is about information security strictly within the digital domain. That is, Information Security takes a holistic view to ensuring all information assets are protected, regardless of format; whereas the more specialised remit of Cyber Security is to secure assets against digital threats.

Thus, the ontologies gathered in the DISO subdomains of information-security and cyber-security should, in theory, have good potential for substantial domain overlap and thus for forming interesting ontology alignment problems.
